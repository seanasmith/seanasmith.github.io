Learning Objectives

  • Utilize content management system to build a website
  • Design journalistic content for the web
  • Strengthen web writing skills
  • Produce photo and video content in web journalism context

Description:

This project will be an original multimedia journalism piece requiring you to conduct interviews, shoot photo and video and build a web journalism piece on Wordpress. You will install Wordpress to your Reclaim Hosting servers and create a subdomain for your story.

Steps:

  1. Create a subdomain and install Wordpress to your server.
  2. Conduct interviews with at least two different people.
  3. Shoot photo and video footage pertaining to your story
  4. Write the story and produce the content
    • At least 1200 words of written content
    • One edited video (length depends on the story you choose. Some may require more video for effective storytelling, some may not)
    • At least five edited photos.
      • Three must be used in a slideshow or collage, two others may appear on their own somewhere in the story
  5. Include at least three widgets or plugins on your site
  6. Post a link to the project in #general on Slack using subdomain
  7. Present the project to class on April 24.
    • What limitations or freedoms did you find when using Wordpress?
    • What were the biggest challenges is reporting and creating content for the web?
    • What would you do differently the next time you create a multimedia journalism piece?

Grading:

Project 3 is worth 200 points. See the rubric for detailed breakdown of grading criteria.

  • A+: Story goes beyond minimum effort in reporting, use of data elements like maps or graphs
  • A: Strong storytelling, effective use of multimedia components, content is organized creatively, effort exhibited in customizing Wordpress theme
  • B: Story shows minimal effort or is missing some components
  • C: Several missing components and poor reporting effort
  • F: Systemic errors, turned in late, critical errors.
